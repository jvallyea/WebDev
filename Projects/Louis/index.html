About the Team


4 Engineering Undergraduates

We've stuck together through thick and thin - and we've come out smarter, wiser, and closer!

Last semester, two of us took ECON 1130 (Mathematical Microeconomics), with Roberto Serrano. Despite being at 9 AM, Professor Serrano made ECON one hell-of-a-class, full of surprises and turns!

But what was perhaps our biggest surprise was when he walked into our 1st class, holding a cane in one hand, and the arm of a graduate student in the other. He was visually impaired, and one of the smartest people we had ever met.

Over the course of the semester, we got to know him better, and learned more about teh difficulties faced by the visually impaired - from things we did without a second thought like checking email and navigating a cafeteria, we quickly learned that technology to aid the visually impaired was limited.

Starting in the Fall of 2017, we began developing Louis. To open-source an increase the accessibility of the technology we were developing, we decided to 3D all of the components of the device (except the motors and slip ring.

We developed a 2-motor system, where one motor moved along a central axis, while the other rotated to allow the Braille to change, creating a dynamic device.

Braille pieces were printed on octagons, where each face had one of 8 permutations of the 3-dot system. Thus, as the octagon rotates, so too does the text being displayed.

A camera attached to a Raspberry pi fed images into Google's Vision API, along with Microsoft's Azure API to summarize the image with a caption under 5 words. This caption was then displayed on Louis' Braille face.

We are currently working to develop a dynamic interface, where Louis can be used to assist the visually impaired in checking social media and email as well.

To find out more, please visit http://www.github.com/jvallyea/Louis or contact me directly.
